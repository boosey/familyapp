# Naming Options — A Perpetual Family Chronicle

*Companion to Family-Chronicle-Vision.md. Replaces the working name "Kith." Brief: evocative/metaphor + invented/coined strategies, warm & intimate tone, top picks availability-checked.*

---

## What the name has to carry

Three threads from the vision doc constrain the name, and the good ones honor all three:

- **The attic, not StoryWorth.** The doc names the real competitor: the unlabeled shoebox, the trunk, the one relative who "knew all the family history" and took it with them. Your quilt / shoebox / trunk metaphors are exactly right — the product *is* the well-kept version of the attic.
- **A living institution, not a one-time gift.** It "outlives any single contributor." The name should feel like something handed down and kept running, not a cute app. That pushes away from disposable, app-store-cute names.
- **Dignity and warmth for elders.** No condescension, no baby-talk. A grandmother should feel proud to say the name out loud.

A note on what to avoid: **Heirloom and Kindred are effectively burned** in this category — multiple live products each (Heirloom, Airloom, MyHeirloom, Heirloom and Tell; OurKindred, Kindred Moments, Kindred Tales). **Keepsake** is taken too. I've steered the shortlist toward names that are still ownable.

## The shortlist (top picks)

These are the ones I'd actually build a brand on. Availability notes are a first-pass web/app-store check, not a legal clearance.

### 1. Vellum *(evocative — recommended)*

The treated calf-skin that medieval manuscripts were written on *because it lasts a thousand years.* It says permanence, craft, and "this is a document meant to outlive us" without being stuffy. Soft sound, warm in the mouth, two syllables. Maps perfectly onto "a perpetual chronicle." A grandmother says it with no friction.

- **Why it fits:** the chronicle-as-lasting-record thesis, the "render a snapshot from a stream that never stops" idea — vellum is the surface a chronicle is kept on.
- **Watch-outs:** there's a well-known book-formatting tool also called Vellum (for authors making ebooks). Different category, but worth a trademark check. The .com is likely taken; a modifier (`vellum.family`, `getvellum`, `vellumco`) is the realistic path.

### 2. Mantel *(evocative)*

The shelf over the hearth where families set the photos, the urn, the wedding picture, the things that matter — the literal altar of family memory in a home. Warm, domestic, dignified. Quietly doubles as "mantle," the thing passed from one generation to the next ("the mantle passes"). That double meaning *is* your product: the hearth-shelf of memory and the handing-down.

- **Why it fits:** hearth warmth + the generational hand-off (Mode 5 stewardship) in one word.
- **Watch-outs:** common English word, so exact .com is unlikely; `mantel.family`, `ourmantel`, `mantelapp` are plausible. Easy to misspell as "mantle" — could be a feature (lean into it) or a support headache.

### 3. Cedarchest / Cedar *(evocative)*

The cedar chest (hope chest, trunk in the attic) is the single most literal version of your metaphor — the trunk where a family stores quilts, letters, and photos, cedar-lined *to protect them for decades.* "Cedar" alone is warm, natural, and clean; "Cedarchest" is unambiguous. The trunk-in-the-attic image is right there.

- **Why it fits:** your trunk/shoebox metaphor, made tangible; cedar literally means "preserved for the long haul."
- **Watch-outs:** "Cedar" is used widely outside this space (lots of companies). "Cedarchest" or "Cedar Trunk" is more ownable than bare "Cedar."

### 4. Trousseau *(evocative — the bold pick)*

The trousseau / hope chest: what a family gathered and handed forward in anticipation of a new life. Gorgeous, distinctive, almost no competition in tech. The risk is the flip side of the charm — it's French, some people won't know how to say or spell it, and it skews feminine/bridal.

- **Why it fits:** the most romantic expression of "gathered and handed down."
- **Watch-outs:** pronunciation ("troo-SOH") and spelling friction with the exact elder audience you most need to win. Beautiful but high-effort.

## Invented / coined options

Coined names trademark cleanly and can own the category outright — the trade-off is you have to *teach* their meaning, and the warm ones below try to make that easy by keeping a real root audible.

### 5. Loomwell

"Loom" (where a quilt/tapestry is woven — your heirloom-quilt metaphor) + "well" (a deep source you draw from; also "wellbeing"). Sounds like a real word, feels handcrafted, and the weaving image maps onto "one chronicle, many threads." Warm, calm, ownable.

### 6. Keepwell

"Keep" (to preserve; also a *keep* — the strong, protected heart of a castle) + "well." Reads as a gentle blessing — "keep well" — which lands beautifully for an elder-facing product. Feels like care, not software.

### 7. Everloom

"Ever" (perpetual — your never-finishing chronicle) + "loom" (the weaving). Says "always weaving, never done" in one word. Slightly more product-y / techy than the others but still warm.

### 8. Marrow

Not invented, but rare enough in this space to feel coined. "In your marrow / family in the bones" — the deepest, most essential part of you, carried in the body and passed down. Short, strong, memorable, a little startling in a good way. Skews more intense/literary than cozy — closer to "this is who we are at the core" than "shoebox of photos." A high-conviction pick if you want gravity over coziness.

### 9. Wend

Old English *to travel / make one's way* (as in "wend your way"), and the root of "went" — literally about journeys across time. Tiny, soft, unusual, and very ownable. Quietly evokes lineage moving through generations. Risk: so abstract it needs a tagline to explain itself.

## How they cluster

If you want **cozy and domestic:** Mantel, Cedarchest, Keepwell.
If you want **timeless and crafted:** Vellum, Loomwell, Trousseau.
If you want **strong and a little surprising:** Marrow, Everloom, Wend.

My single recommendation is **Vellum** for the headline brand — it carries permanence, craft, and warmth at once and an elder can say it without thinking — with **Mantel** as the strongest backup if you want the hearth/hand-off meaning more explicit, and **Loomwell** as the safest-to-trademark coined option.
