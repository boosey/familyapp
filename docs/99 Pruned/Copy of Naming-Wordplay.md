# Naming — Wordplay Worksheet: Saga · Family · Trunk · Quilt · Heirloom

*A build-from-the-roots round. For each seed word: synonyms, roots/fragments, and then derivations, expansions, and portmanteaus built from them. Conflict notes inline — names already live in this space are struck or flagged.*

**Live in this space already (avoid):** Heirloom, Airloom, HeirLoft, Kinloom, Kindred, Saga (app), Sagewell (banking), Loomage (furnishings), Trove, Locket, Keepsake. These shaped which blends below survive.

---

## SEED 1 — SAGA

**Synonyms / kin:** epic, chronicle, legend, lore, tale, yarn, annal, edda, fable.
**Roots / fragments:** *saga-* (Old Norse "what is said," same root as "say"); *-saga*.

**Derivations & expansions:**
- **Sagable** — saga + -able; "everything here is worth a saga." Soft, brandable.
- **Sagely / Sagewise** — saga + the wisdom pun. Warm, a little knowing.
- **Sagestry** — saga + tapestry. Blends the telling *and* the weaving (ties Saga to the quilt seed). Distinctive; appears clear.
- **Sagara / Sagora** — invented expansions; pure sound, ownable, place-like.
- **Annal / Annaly** — from *annals* (yearly records); "the family's annals." Quiet, institutional.
- **Edda** — the Norse book of collected lore + lineages (flagged before; still one of the cleanest "means-exactly-this" words).

---

## SEED 2 — FAMILY

**Synonyms / kin:** kin, kith, kindred, clan, folk, kinfolk, house, stock, lineage, brood, hearth, tribe.
**Roots / fragments:** *famil-* (L. *familia*, household); *kin-*; *-kin* (also "little/dear," as in *kinfolk*); *kith*.

**Derivations & expansions:**
- **Familiar / Familiary** — *familiar* hides "family" inside it and means "intimately known." **Familiary** (family + library/aviary) = "the place the family is kept." Distinctive, ownable, warm-but-not-soft. Appears clear.
- **Famish? no.** Skip — wrong connotation.
- **Kinfold** — kinfolk + fold (to enfold, and a *fold* shelters a flock). Protective, warm, ownable.
- **Kithen** — kith + -en (like *hearth/kitchen* warmth); a coined "place of kith." Soft, homey.
- **Kinloom** — *taken* (live app). But it points the way → **Kithloom** (kith + loom): your weaving metaphor + chosen family. Appears clear and is a near-cousin of your old "Kith."
- **Folkkeep / Folkloom** — folk + keep/loom. "Folklore" is hiding in folk+lore.
- **Househeld** — house + held + "household." The family record, held. Sturdy.

---

## SEED 3 — TRUNK

**Synonyms / kin:** chest, coffer, hope chest, cedar chest, steamer, footlocker, ark, vault, casket (avoid — morbid).
**Roots / fragments:** *trunk* also = the main stem of a tree (lineage!) and the torso/core; *coffer-*; *ark-*.

**Derivations & expansions:**
- **Trunkful** — "a trunkful of stories." Homey, plain, ownable.
- **Trunkline** — *taken* (industrial). But "the trunk line" = the main line of descent (tree trunk → lineage) is a lovely double meaning; a modifier could rescue it.
- **Coffer / Cofferly** — a coffer is a strongbox for treasure *and* (architecturally) a recessed panel in a grand ceiling. Rich, uncommon, dignified.
- **Ark** — the vessel that carries what must survive across a long passage (Noah's ark, Ark of the Covenant). Short, weighty, mythic, very ownable. Strong spine.
- **Arkive** — ark + archive. The carrying-vessel *and* the record, in one coined word. Distinctive; check (some scattered use).
- **Cedarn** — archaic adjective "made of cedar"; coined-feeling, warm, the chest's protection without the literal "chest."

---

## SEED 4 — QUILT

**Synonyms / kin:** patchwork, coverlet, counterpane, comforter, stitchwork, eiderdown, crazy quilt, medley.
**Roots / fragments:** *quilt-*; *patch-*; *stitch-*; *piece-* (to "piece" a quilt = to join scraps into a whole — perfect for your "many stories, one chronicle").

**Derivations & expansions:**
- **Patchwork / Patchwork.family** — the literal "many pieces, one whole." Common word; needs a modifier.
- **Coverlet** — a woven bed covering passed down generations; soft, period, distinctive, appears clear. Pretty but quiet.
- **Counterpane** — antique word for a quilted coverlet; unusual, characterful, ownable. Risk: "counter" reads techy/mismatched.
- **Pieceworth / Piecework** — "piecing" the family together; *piecework* exists (labor term) so lean to **Pieceworth** or **Pieced**.
- **Stitchright / Stitchwell / Stitchkin** — stitch (joining scraps into one cloth) + well/kin. The making-whole-from-scraps metaphor = your synthesis engine. Warm, crafted, with a little spine.
- **Crazyquilt** — the real term for an improvised patchwork of mismatched scraps; charming and very "the messy attic, made whole." Bold, memorable.

## SEED 5 — HEIRLOOM

*(The word itself is burned — many live products. But its parts are gold: HEIR + LOOM.)*

**Fragments:** *heir-* (the one who inherits); *-loom* (the weaving frame — and literally where "heirloom" comes from: an *heir* + a *loom*, a tool passed down).

**Derivations & expansions (recombining the parts so it's NOT "heirloom"):**
- **Kithloom** — kith + loom (also under Family). My favorite of the loom-blends: keeps the inheritance-weaving idea, drops the burned word, nods to your original "Kith."
- **Loomwell / Loomkin / Loomward** — loom + well/kin/ward (toward, in keeping of). Crafted, rooted.
- **Heirward** — heir + -ward (toward/keeper of); "in the keeping of the heirs." Dignified, ownable, sidesteps "loom." Strong.
- **Heirling** — heir + -ling (the young heir, a descendant); soft, human, distinctive.
- **Bequest / Bequeath** — what is handed down by will (ties to your Mode 5 "story will"). **Bequest** is weighty and exact; common-ish word, check.
- **Legacy → Legacye / Legara** — *Legacy* is overused, but invented stretches (Legara) escape it.

## PORTMANTEAU LAB — blending across seeds

Combining roots from two seeds at once (the most ownable, most distinctive route):

- **Sagestry** = saga + tapestry → *telling + weaving.* (Saga × Quilt)
- **Kithloom** = kith + loom → *chosen family + inheritance-weaving.* (Family × Heirloom)
- **Arkive** = ark + archive → *the vessel that carries the record.* (Trunk × record)
- **Familiary** = family + library → *the place the family is kept.* (Family × archive)
- **Heirward** = heir + ward → *in the keeping of those who inherit.* (Heirloom × steward)
- **Stitchkin** = stitch + kin → *piecing the family whole.* (Quilt × Family)
- **Cofferkin** = coffer + kin → *the family's strongbox.* (Trunk × Family)
- **Sagahold / Sagakeep** = saga + hold/keep → *where the family saga is kept.* (Saga × steward)
- **Trunkloom** = trunk(line of descent) + loom → *the main line, woven.* (Trunk × Heirloom)

## My picks from this round

**Most distinctive + ownable (coined):** **Sagestry**, **Arkive**, **Familiary**, **Kithloom**.
**Most spine (weighty real words):** **Ark**, **Coffer**, **Bequest**, **Heirward**.
**Warmest with craft:** **Stitchkin**, **Coverlet**, **Loomwell**.

If I had to shortlist three to pressure-test: **Sagestry** (telling + weaving, unique, ties two of your seeds), **Arkive** (vessel + record, weighty and clever without being cute), and **Kithloom** (inheritance-weaving + a callback to your original Kith).
