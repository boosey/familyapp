Multi-Family Identity & Data Model

Families are overlapping webs, not clean trees. A marriage merges two chronicles; a divorce splits one; a child belongs to several lineages at once. The foundational Phase 0 decision.

The vision asked: one archive, or many families? The answer is both — by separating them: one person, one archive, many memberships, per-story visibility.

The false choice · and the key insight that dissolves it

Too rigid — one clean tree

Each person belongs to exactly one family. A grandmother loved by two families must pick one; a child of divorce can't be represented.

⛔ Fights reality — breaks at the first marriage

vs

Too loose — everything shared

Stories flow freely across families. An ex-spouse sees what was never meant for them.

⛔ Privacy breaks — leakage across boundaries

→

Separate identity from membership

A person is one continuous self who participates in many family contexts — and chooses what's shared in each.

✓ Holds the messy human truth

The model · three separate dials

P

Identity

"Who a person is"

One permanent, singular Person. Owns their stories, voice & consent — independent of any family. Answers: carry one archive through life.

M

Membership

"Which families they're in"

A plural, revocable link from Person to Family, carrying a role. Add on marriage/birth; end on divorce/estrangement. Answers: belong to many families at once.

S

Visibility

"What's shared where"

A Story is authored once, owned by the Person, and surfaced into families by audience tier — never copied. Answers: what's shared where.

Every messy case becomes ordinary · a change in membership or visibility

Marriage

Gain a membership; keep the existing one. One person, now in two families.

Divorce

Memberships end. No identity or stories deleted — the person carries their archive out intact.

Blended family

Memberships in multiple chronicles & branches. Never "pick a side."

Adoption

Adoptive membership, plus birth-family ties only if chosen & consented.

Estrangement

Pause or scope a membership. Visibility is a dial, not a demolition.

Death

Identity persists; the story will travels with the person across all families.

Phase 0 · build the atoms, defer the heavy machinery

✓ Build now

  • Person — first-class, permanent, singular; owns stories & consent
  • Membership — separate, plural, revocable, role-carrying
  • Story owned by Person; audience tier evaluated per membership
  • The three dials cleanly separated — the seam that makes edge cases ordinary

⏳ Defer (leave seams)

  • Full federation (Model C) — only at scale, if families demand sovereignty
  • Cross-chronicle conflict resolution & sync
  • Deep multi-lineage genealogy — the tree deepens in Phase 3 enrichment
