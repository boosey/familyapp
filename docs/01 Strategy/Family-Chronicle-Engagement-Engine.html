The Engagement Engine

The heartbeat that keeps a perpetual chronicle alive between big moments. A rescue mission has built-in urgency; a perpetual chronicle must manufacture its own reasons to return — or it becomes the digital shoebox: preserved but dead.

The core retention loop · every trigger must complete it

1

A signal occurs

A date, a record, a location, a gap, a relative's contribution becomes relevant.

→
2

Surfaced gently

Routed to the right person, framed warmly, at a tolerable cadence — not a blast.

→
3

Low-effort action

Answer one question, tap one share, ask one thing, confirm one detail.

→
4

Visible reward

The story grows, a loop closes, someone's delighted — and it feeds the next signal.

↻  Self-feeding: beat 4 generates the next beat 1

In a family system, one person's reward is another's signal: Eleanor's new story is Sofia's notification; Sofia's question is Eleanor's next prompt. The engine runs on the family's own warmth — not manufactured gamification.

The trigger catalog · five signal sources

Group A

Time-based

  • Anniversary prompts — weddings, births, losses
  • "This week in family history" weekly digest
  • Seasonal & holiday recollection + capture
  • Reminiscence-bump sensory prompts (ages 10–30)

The calendar is a free, infinite signal source.

Group B

Discovery-based

  • Record-match — "here's the ship from 1921"
  • Contradiction & gap detection
  • "Day in history" context cards

The unfair advantage. No shoebox finds things for you.

Group C

Mystery & quest

  • Collaborative family mysteries — "who is this?"
  • Gap-as-invitation — holes become quests
  • Geolocation memory triggers

An open question pulls people back. Curiosity is renewable.

Group D

Social

  • Question→answer loop (the relay's core)
  • New-contribution notifications
  • Milestone & membership events
  • Nudge-the-elder (initiator-driven)

Cheapest & stickiest — a real human made the reason.

Group E

Legacy & forward-time

  • Time-capsule releases (an 18th birthday)
  • Present-tense capture nudges (Mode 2)
  • Auto editions & documentaries

Unique to a perpetual chronicle: time runs both ways.

Orchestration · the conductor's rules

One voice, one cadence per person

Each persona has a tolerated frequency. Respect per-person budgets; silence is always an option.

Bundle, batch, prefer pull over push

Most triggers accumulate into a digest the person chooses to open. Push only for time-sensitive warmth.

Close the loop or cut it

If it doesn't lead to a low-effort action with a visible reward that feeds the next signal, it's noise.

★ The elder's experience is sacrosanct

No trigger may ever make Eleanor feel nagged, corrected, or surveilled. Everything reaches her as a decline-able invitation.

Sequencing · build in the order that compounds

First

Social loop + weekly digest

Runs on the family's own warmth and existing content. Cheap, immediately sticky. The answered-question loop is highest leverage.

→

Second

Discovery triggers

Once external data is wired in, record-matches and gap detection become the moat no competitor can match.

→

Third

Mysteries + geolocation

Deepen multi-player engagement once there's enough chronicle to mine.

→

Fourth

Legacy + forward-time

Time-capsules and auto-editions presuppose a mature, trusted, well-governed chronicle.
